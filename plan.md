# Handheld PC Gaming Console Knowledge Base — Execution Plan

## Project Overview
Build an exhaustive Tech Knowledge Base for Handheld PC Gaming Consoles — a niche authority site with 50-product comparison database, 30-article content silo, and monetization hub.

## Skill Loading Strategy
- **Stage 1**: `deep-research-swarm` — Research 2026-current specs for "Big 5" consoles and 50+ accessories
- **Stage 2**: `report-writing` — Draft 30 articles (10 Blogs, 10 How-Tos, 10 Versus) using research data
- **Stage 3**: `vibecoding-webapp-swarm` — Build interactive comparison engine website
- **Stage 4**: `docx` — Final deliverable compilation

## Stage 1: Deep Research (Parallel Swarm)
### 1a — Console Research (Big 5)
- Steam Deck OLED, ASUS ROG Ally X, Lenovo Legion Go S, MSI Claw 8 AI+, Nintendo Switch 2
- Focus: 2026 models only, technical specs, pricing, availability

### 1b — Accessories Research (50 Products)
- 10 Power Solutions (65W+ power banks, GaN chargers)
- 10 Storage (UHS-II SD cards, 2230 SSDs)
- 10 Docks/Hubs (USB-C, HDMI 2.1, Ethernet)
- 10 Peripherals (Hall-Effect controllers, portable monitors, grips/cases)
- 10 Consoles (Pro/OLED/Lite variants of Big 5)

### 1c — Monetization & Software Research
- Affiliate programs: Amazon Associates, Best Buy, Walmart, JSAUX, Humble Bundle
- Software tools: EmuDeck, Decky Loader, ProtonDB, Handheld Companion
- Price comparison APIs/widgets

## Stage 2: Content Writing (Parallel, 10 batches of 3 articles)
### Batch 1-3: Blog Posts (Trends & Traffic)
### Batch 4-6: How-To Guides (Authority & Retention)
### Batch 7-10: Versus Comparisons (High-Conversion)

## Stage 3: Comparison Engine & Website Build
- Interactive 50-product comparison table
- Price comparison widgets
- Filterable/searchable database
- Responsive design

## Stage 4: Assembly & Delivery
- Compile all articles into structured knowledge base
- Generate final .docx deliverable
- Deploy comparison engine website

## Quality Standards
- Tone: Deeply technical, unsponsored, enthusiast-to-enthusiast
- Format: H2/H3 headers, bold specs, Markdown tables
- Accuracy: 2026 standards (120Hz+, VRR, 80Wh+ batteries)
- No outdated hardware (no base Steam Deck LCD, no original Ally Z1)
