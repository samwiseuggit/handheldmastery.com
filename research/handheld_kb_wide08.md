# Facet: Monetization & Affiliate Programs

## Key Findings

- **Amazon Associates** pays 1-4% on electronics/gaming hardware with a 24-hour cookie — the baseline program for most tech affiliates due to massive catalog and high conversion rates, but rates have declined steadily since the 2020 cuts [^240^].
- **JSAUX** offers the best direct accessory-brand affiliate program at **7% commission with 30-day cookies**, available via both UpPromote and Impact [^235^] [^239^].
- **Best Buy** temporarily slashed affiliate payouts to **0% baseline** in April 2025 due to "broader economic factors," making it unreliable despite its brand authority [^243^].
- **Humble Bundle** pays **8-10% commission** with 7-30 day cookies on game bundles — excellent for digital game content monetization [^232^] [^234^].
- **B&H Photo** offers **2-8% commission** with 60-hour to 30-day cookies and 3-4x higher EPC than Amazon, making it the best Amazon alternative for high-ticket electronics [^346^].
- **Anker** offers **8% commission with 30-day cookies** through Webgains, with rates up to 15% in some European markets — one of the highest-paying electronics accessory programs [^302^] [^308^].
- **SteelSeries** offers up to **8% commission** with 30-day cookies — one of the highest base rates for gaming peripherals [^387^].
- **ASUS** only pays **1-3%** on systems/laptops with higher rates on accessories, and accessories/bundles/phones are excluded from commissions in some regions [^341^] [^347^].
- **Walmart** offers only **1% on electronics/video games** but has a 3-day cookie (longer than Amazon's 24 hours), making it a weak choice for tech-focused affiliates [^264^] [^268^].
- **FTC compliance** requires clear, conspicuous disclosures placed close to affiliate links — footer-only disclosures are insufficient under current guidance [^301^] [^313^] [^402^].

## Amazon Associates Deep Dive

### Current Commission Rates (2025-2026)

| Category | Commission Rate |
|----------|----------------|
| Amazon Games | 20% (highest priority) |
| Luxury Beauty | 10% |
| Digital Music / Physical Music | 5% |
| Kitchen / Automotive / Handmade | 4.5% |
| All Other Categories | 4% |
| Toys / Furniture / Home / Outdoors / Headphones | 3% |
| PC, PC Components, DVD & Blu-Ray | 2.5% |
| Televisions / Digital Video Games | 2% |
| Physical Video Games & Consoles | 1% |
| Gift Cards / Wireless Plans / Alcoholic Beverages | 0% |

**Key insight:** PC components at 2.5% and general electronics at 3% are the most relevant rates for handheld gaming accessories. Physical consoles earn only 1% [^240^] [^241^].

### Historical Context
- **2012:** Average rate ~9.25% (peak)
- **2017:** Shifted from volume-based to flat category rates
- **April 2020:** Major cuts during COVID — furniture dropped 8% → 3%, grocery 5% → 1%
- **2025:** Amazon Games introduced at 20% (strategic growth category); Amazon Haul at 7% [^240^] [^233^]

### Optimization Strategies
- **Cross-category commissions:** When a user clicks for a low-commission console but buys a high-commission item (or multiple items), you earn on the entire cart [^344^]
- **Boosted periods:** Amazon doubles commissions in key categories before Prime Day (April, July) — beauty, kitchen, home essentials see 3-6% rate increases [^230^]
- **Native Commerce Advertising (2025):** New program that rewards publishers for driving traffic even without purchases [^240^]
- **Prime Eligibility:** Products with Prime shipping convert significantly better; JSAUX products on Amazon benefit from this [^229^]

### Link Types and Tools
- **SiteStripe:** Amazon's in-browser link tool (note: image code was deprecated in 2023; now requires API for product images) [^408^]
- **Product Advertising API:** Required for real-time price, image, and availability data
- **Amazon Attribution:** For tracking off-Amazon traffic

## Best Buy Affiliate Analysis

### Program Structure
- **Network:** Impact (formerly Rakuten Linkshare until 2019) [^236^]
- **Commission:** 0.5-1% on most products (5% bounty on wireless plans only) [^12^]
- **Cookie:** 1 day (24 hours) — same as Amazon [^12^]
- **Attribution:** Last-click model

### Critical Issues (2025)
- **Payout slashed to 0%:** In April 2025, Best Buy reduced baseline affiliate payouts to "temporary 0%" due to "broader economic factors," with payments also delayed in March 2025 [^243^]
- **Low EPC:** Average earnings per click is $0.011-$0.015 vs Amazon's ~$0.05 [^236^]
- **Low conversion:** Average conversion rate 0.47%, average basket $203.53 [^236^]
- **Gaming exclusions:** Gaming hardware, gift cards, marketplace third-party items, and certain open-box products are frequently excluded [^12^]

### Strategic Verdict
Best Buy should be a **supplemental** program only. Its 1-day cookie makes it unsuitable for electronics where consumers research for days or weeks. Use only when Best Buy has exclusive bundles or better prices than competitors [^12^].

## Content Monetization Strategy

### High-Converting Content Types for Gaming Hardware

| Content Type | Conversion Rate | Best For |
|-------------|----------------|----------|
| Product reviews | 2-4% | Individual product deep-dives |
| Comparison guides | 4-7% | "Best X for Y" decision-stage content |
| "Best of" listicles | 3-5% | SEO-driven traffic, broad keywords |
| How-to / setup tutorials | 2-3% | Top-of-funnel, building trust |
| PC build guides | 5-8% | Multiple affiliate clicks per article |
| Bundle recommendations | 6-8% | AOV optimization |

### Content Strategy Framework

**1. The "Bundle Effect"**
When a user clicks for one product but adds matching accessories, commissions multiply. Example: Logitech G Pro mouse ($120) + mousepad ($40) + headset = $200-400 order earning $20-40 commission vs. $3 from a single game sale [^340^].

**2. Evergreen vs. Timely Content**
- **Evergreen:** "Best mechanical keyboards" — generates passive income for 12-18 months with gamers upgrading every 2-3 years
- **Timely:** New GPU launches, console releases, Prime Day deals — massive traffic spikes requiring quick content turnaround [^339^]

**3. Content Clustering**
- Pillar: "Complete Handheld Gaming Setup Guide"
- Clusters: Individual reviews for docks, cases, chargers, screen protectors
- Budget segments: Budget / Mid-range / Enthusiast tiers [^339^]

**4. Peak Season Timing**
- **January-February:** Tax refund season (young adults with extra cash)
- **August-September:** Back-to-school
- **November:** Black Friday / Cyber Monday (absolute peak for hardware sales) [^340^]

**5. Cross-Platform Strategy**
Gaming hardware reviews translate exceptionally well to YouTube:
- Video reviews attract display ad revenue + affiliate links
- Setup/unboxing videos achieve 68% conversion lift for accessories [^229^]
- Cross-promote between blog and YouTube for maximum reach

## FTC Compliance

### Core Requirements

The FTC requires **clear, conspicuous, and unavoidable** disclosures whenever a material relationship exists between a creator and a brand [^301^] [^313^].

**Material relationships include:**
- Affiliate links or commission-based referrals
- Free products or review samples
- Paid sponsorships
- Discounts, perks, or early access
- Family, personal, or employment ties [^301^]

### What "Clear and Conspicuous" Means

| Required | NOT Sufficient |
|----------|---------------|
| Plain language: "I earn a commission" | Vague hashtags: "#spon" or "#collab" |
| Placed where users see it without clicking | Hidden behind "read more" or inside long captions |
| Appears early in the post/video | Buried after hashtags or in footer only |
| Visual + verbal disclosure in video | Platform-provided tools alone (can supplement but not replace) |
| Before any affiliate links appear | Disclosure only on a separate policy page |

### Disclosure Templates for Tech Reviews

**Short inline (for blog posts):**
> "As an Amazon Associate, I earn from qualifying purchases."

**Standard version:**
> "When you purchase through links on our site, we may earn an affiliate commission. Here's how it works." [^406^]

**Review-specific:**
> "We may earn affiliate commission if you purchase items through links on our site. [Product name] was provided by the manufacturer for review purposes." [^304^]

**Comprehensive:**
> "Disclosure: This post contains affiliate links, meaning I get a commission if you decide to make a purchase through my links, at no cost to you."

### Amazon Associates Specific Requirements
Amazon's Operating Agreement **requires** affiliates to include an Amazon-specific disclosure. Example:
> "[Site name] is a participant in the Amazon Services LLC Associates Program, an affiliate advertising program designed to provide a means for sites to earn advertising fees by advertising and linking to Amazon.com." [^402^]

### Placement Best Practices

| Location | Effectiveness | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Top of page (above fold)** | Highest | First thing users see; Tech Radar uses this approach [^406^] |
| **Inline near affiliate links** | High | Best for compliance; "This is an affiliate link" in parentheses |
| **Fixed sidebar** | Medium | Visible on every post; good supplement [^402^] |
| **Footer** | Low | Not sufficient alone; use as supplement only |
| **Dedicated disclosure page** | Reference only | Link to it from inline disclosures; not sufficient alone |

### Enforcement Examples
- **Amazon Affiliate Links (2023):** FTC issued warning letters to influencers promoting products with Amazon affiliate links without disclosure [^301^]
- **Child-Directed YouTube (2024):** Increased FTC scrutiny on toy/family creators failing to disclose paid promotions [^301^]
- Penalties range from warning letters to fines of **$10,000 to millions** [^310^]

## E-E-A-T for Tech Review Content

### Why E-E-A-T Matters for YMYL Tech Content

While tech reviews aren't "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL) in the traditional sense (health/finance), **high-ticket electronics purchases** (>$500) where buyers rely on your recommendations create similar trust expectations. Google treats buying guides with the same scrutiny [^366^] [^370^].

### E-E-A-T Implementation for Handheld Gaming Reviews

**Experience:**
- Document hands-on testing time with each product
- Include photos/videos of products in actual use (not just manufacturer images)
- Describe real-world usage scenarios (e.g., "I used this dock for 40 hours with my Steam Deck")
- Include troubleshooting tips from actual experience [^366^]

**Expertise:**
- Create detailed author bios with tech credentials
- Use Person schema markup with credentials, professional affiliations
- Cite technical specifications from manufacturer sources
- Explain technical concepts in accessible language [^367^]

**Authoritativeness:**
- Build backlinks from tech community discussions (Reddit, forums)
- Get mentioned/linked by other tech sites
- Maintain active presence in handheld gaming communities
- Publish consistently on the niche topic [^367^]

**Trustworthiness:**
- HTTPS mandatory
- Clear privacy policy, terms of service, editorial guidelines
- Transparent disclosure of all affiliate relationships
- "Last updated" timestamps on all reviews
- Correct outdated information promptly
- Link to authoritative external sources [^367^] [^370^]

### Technical E-E-A-T Signals

```
Schema markup to implement:
- Article schema with author and publisher properties
- Person schema for author profiles (with credentials)
- Review schema with author expertise indicators
- Organization schema for site identity
- FAQPage schema for common questions
```

### Content Quality Standards
- Cross-reference all factual claims with manufacturer specs
- Implement expert review workflow for high-ticket recommendations
- Document verification sources
- Establish regular update protocols (quarterly for evergreen content) [^366^]
- Include both pros AND cons — balanced reviews outperform pure promotions
- Specify when a product was provided by the manufacturer vs. purchased independently
