### Key Findings

- **The JSAUX Upgraded 5-in-1 Docking Station is the best overall dock for most users in 2026**, offering HDMI 2.1 (4K@120Hz), 100W PD, Gigabit Ethernet, and broad compatibility at ~$45 — less than half the price of Valve's official dock [^14^][^21^].
- **The JSAUX HB0604 is the top dock with M.2 SSD expansion**, featuring a built-in NVMe M.2 slot that supports up to 4TB of additional storage, turning a handheld into a desktop-like experience [^13^][^19^].

### 4K TV Docked Gaming Performance

Docked gaming performance is limited by the handheld's APU, not the dock itself. The dock only provides the video output pathway.

| Handheld | Docked Resolution | Typical FPS ( demanding games) | Typical FPS (older/indie) | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|-------|
| **Steam Deck OLED** | 720p–1080p (internal render), up to 4K output | 30–60 fps at 1080p Low-Medium | Up to 120 fps at 1080p/4K | 15W APU; use FSR upscaling for 4K output [^98^] |
| **ROG Ally X** | 1080p (internal), up to 4K output | 40–60 fps at 1080p Medium | Up to 120 fps | 30W Turbo Mode needs 100W charger [^21^] |
| **Legion Go 2** | 1080p–1440p, up to 4K output | 40–60 fps at 1080p | Up to 120 fps | Larger screen may mean better thermal headroom |
| **MSI Claw 8 AI+** | 1080p, up to 4K output | 35–55 fps at 1080p | Up to 120 fps | TB4 enables eGPU for true 4K gaming [^63^] |

**4K Gaming Reality Check:**
- Native 4K gaming is **not feasible** on any current handheld APU for demanding titles [^98^]
- **Recommended approach:** Render at 720p–1080p internally, use FSR 2.0/FSR 3 Frame Generation to upscale to 4K output [^91^]
- For 4K@120Hz output, you need: (1) HDMI 2.1 dock (BenQ GR10, JSAUX Upgraded), (2) 120Hz TV/monitor, (3) game that can hit 120fps — mostly limited to indie/older titles [^17^][^98^]
- Steam Deck specifically: target **30fps at 4K** for AAA games using FSR upscaling, similar to PS4 Pro performance [^98^]

**Recommended Game Settings (Docked):**
- **Baldur's Gate 3:** 1080p@30-35fps (quality) or 720p@40-50fps (performance) [^91^]
- **Cyberpunk 2077:** 30fps (higher quality) or 60fps (smoother) at 1080p with FSR 3 [^91^]
- **Elden Ring:** 720p–1080p, Medium settings, lock to 30fps [^91^]
- **HELLDIVERS 2:** 720p for external display, Medium settings [^91^]

### Thunderbolt 4 Advantage (MSI Claw 8 AI+)

The MSI Claw 8 AI+ is the only major handheld with **dual Thunderbolt 4 ports**, providing significant docking advantages:

| Feature | USB-C (Steam Deck/ROG/Legion) | Thunderbolt 4 (MSI Claw) |
|---------|-------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Max Bandwidth** | 10Gbps (USB 3.2) | **40Gbps** |
| **Display Output** | 1× 4K@120Hz (DP Alt Mode) | **2× 4K@60Hz or 1× 8K** |
| **eGPU Support** | No (USB4 on some, but limited) | **Yes — full eGPU support** |
| **PCIe Lanes** | Shared with USB | **Dedicated PCIe 3.0 x4** |
| **Power Delivery** | Up to 100W | **Up to 100W** |

**Real-World Impact:**
- The MSI Claw can connect to a **Razer Thunderbolt 4 Dock Chroma** ($305) for dual 4K displays + 3× TB4 ports + RGB [^57^]
- **eGPU support:** MSI Claw + Aoostar AG02 eGPU dock + RTX 5070 achieved desktop-grade 4K gaming via USB4/Thunderbolt [^63^]
- Bandwidth for eGPU: USB4/TB4 provides ~32Gbps effective for PCIe, compared to ~10Gbps on standard USB-C docks [^63^]
- Other docks (UGREEN, iVANKY 8-in-1) work fine with MSI Claw via standard USB-C, but TB4 unlocks the full potential [^57^]

**Note:** The Ivanky FusionDock Max 1, despite its Thunderbolt 4 branding, is **NOT compatible** with the MSI Claw or any Windows/PC handheld — it is Apple Silicon Mac-only [^25^].

### What to Look for When Buying a Dock

1. **HDMI Version:** 2.0 supports 4K@60Hz; 2.1 supports 4K@120Hz with VRR — essential for premium TVs
2. **Power Delivery:** Minimum 45W for basic charging; 65W+ for Turbo Mode on ROG Ally; 100W ideal for future-proofing
3. **USB-A Ports:** 2-3 ports minimum for controller + keyboard/mouse + external storage
4. **Ethernet:** Gigabit (1Gbps) standard; 2.5Gb on premium docks for faster downloads
5. **SSD Expansion:** M.2 NVMe slot only on select docks (JSAUX HB0604) — useful for large game libraries
6. **Cooling:** Active cooling fans help with sustained performance but add bulk
7. **Handheld Fit:** Check if dock accommodates your specific handheld, especially with cases
