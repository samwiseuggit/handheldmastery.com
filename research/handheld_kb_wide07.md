### Key Findings

- **The global RAM/NAND shortage ("RAMageddon") is the dominant market force in 2026**, with DRAM contract prices surging 58-63% in Q2 2026 and NAND flash up 70-75%, causing massive price hikes across all handheld gaming PCs [^214^][^203^]
- **Steam Deck OLED is effectively unavailable** at most major retailers due to the memory shortage; Valve has acknowledged "intermittent" out-of-stock issues and discontinued the LCD 256GB model [^261^][^244^]
- **ROG Ally X remains the most stable value proposition** at its $999.99 MSRP at Best Buy, with open-box units as low as $809.99, while competitors have raised prices significantly [^143^][^306^]
- **Lenovo Legion Go 2 has experienced the most extreme price hikes**, with the 2TB Z2 Extreme model now at $2,849.99 on Lenovo's website (up 92% from $1,479.99 MSRP) [^166^][^174^]
- **Lenovo Legion Go S prices have nearly doubled**, with the SteamOS Z1 Extreme model jumping from $829.99 to $1,579.99 at Best Buy as of late April 2026 [^302^][^169^]
- **MSI Claw 8 AI+ has risen from $899 MSRP to $1,099.99-$1,299.99** at most retailers, with the new Glacier Blue edition priced at ~$1,520 [^297^][^164^]
- **Refurbished Steam Deck OLED units from Valve offer the best entry-level value** at $439 (512GB) and $519 (1TB) with full warranty, but stock is extremely limited [^283^][^285^]
- **Best Buy open-box ROG Ally X at $809.99-$869.99** represents the best value for a premium Windows handheld currently available [^306^][^303^]
- **B&H Photo is a key alternative retailer** for Legion Go 2, still selling the 2TB model at $1,849.99 while other retailers are sold out or priced much higher [^307^][^308^]
- **UK pricing** shows the ROG Ally X at £799 and Steam Deck OLED at £449 (512GB) / £529 (1TB), with Australian pricing at AU$899 (512GB OLED) / AU$1,049 (1TB OLED) [^276^][^204^]

### Price Trend Analysis

#### Overall Direction: **SHARPLY UPWARD** across all models

**Key Drivers:**
1. **Global RAM/NAND Shortage ("RAMageddon"):** AI data center demand has caused memory manufacturers (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) to redirect capacity from consumer to enterprise products [^210^]
2. **DRAM prices surged 90-95% QoQ in Q1 2026**, with TrendForce forecasting additional 58-63% increases in Q2 2026 [^214^][^203^]
3. **NAND flash prices jumped 55-60% in Q1 2026**, with 70-75% additional increases projected for Q2 [^214^]
4. **Western Digital sold out its entire 2026 storage capacity** by mid-February 2026 [^258^]
